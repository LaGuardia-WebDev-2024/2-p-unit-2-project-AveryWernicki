Edinbrugh

the fist place youll explore in edinbrugh is the historic old town.

here, you can shop at themed shops, explore comedy clubs, go to pubs and see the sights including a funny dog statue and a unicorn.

once you have moved on from the old town, you can continue up the royal mile to edinbrugh castle.



when exploring the castle, you can either explore yourself, or take a guided tour. when taking a tour the historical significance of the castle on the hill is explained deeply, and i found to be a valuable asset to the viewing. choosing what tour is up to you, as you can choose a witch themed tour or something more conventional. before going however, i feel inclined to tell you it is a contant uphill, and can be very steep.

the highlands

the highlands of scotland are not only historical, but comepletly unique. from almost treeless mountains, to nearly constant rain and rolling green hills, its a wonder to behold.
to explore, you can either take a walking holiday with a group, or figure the trails out on your own.

Isle of Skye

from wedding velues to beutiful coastlines to ginger fluffy cows, the isle of skye is a remote dreamland for most.

the isle of skye is a popular island off the coast of scotland, and is known for its countryside wonder and easy living.
